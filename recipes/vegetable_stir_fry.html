Vegetable Stir-Fry

Description

Vegetable stir-fry is a vibrant and nutritious dish featuring an array of colorful vegetables cooked quickly over high heat. Typically prepared in a wok or skillet, the vegetables are cut into bite-sized pieces and tossed together with aromatic ingredients like garlic and ginger. A splash of soy sauce adds savory flavor, while optional toppings like sesame seeds and green onions provide texture and freshness. This versatile dish allows for endless customization, making it easy to incorporate seasonal produce or personal preferences. With its quick cooking time and healthful ingredients, vegetable stir-fry is a popular choice for a delicious and satisfying meal that can be enjoyed on its own or paired with rice or noodles.

Ingredients

  • Assorted vegetables (such as bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, snap peas, onions)
  • Garlic, minced
  • Ginger, minced (optional)
  • Soy sauce
  • Vegetable oil or sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: sesame seeds, green onions

Steps

  1. Prepare the vegetables by washing and chopping them into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add minced garlic and ginger to the skillet and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Add the chopped vegetables to the skillet and stir-fry until they are tender-crisp, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Drizzle soy sauce over the vegetables and toss to coat evenly.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the stir-fried vegetables hot, optionally topped with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.
