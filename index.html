Description

Scrambled eggs are a classic breakfast dish made by whisking eggs, often with a bit of milk or cream, and then cooking them in a pan over medium heat while gently stirring until they're softly set. The result is a creamy, fluffy texture with a mild flavor that can be seasoned with salt, pepper, and various optional ingredients like herbs, cheese, or vegetables for added flavor and texture.

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Butter or oil (for cooking)
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Crack Eggs: Crack the desired number of eggs into a bowl. Typically, you might use 2-3 eggs per serving.
  2. Whisk Eggs: Using a fork or whisk, beat the eggs until the yolks and whites are well combined. If you like, you can add a splash of milk or cream at this stage for creamier eggs.
  3. Season Eggs: Season the beaten eggs with a pinch of salt and a grind of black pepper. You can also add other seasonings or herbs at this point, such as chopped chives or parsley.
  4. Preheat Pan: Place a non-stick skillet or frying pan over medium heat. Allow it to heat up for a minute or two.
  5. Add Butter or Oil: Once the pan is hot, add a small amount of butter or oil (about 1 teaspoon per egg) to the pan. Allow the butter to melt and coat the bottom of the pan evenly.
  6. Pour Eggs into Pan: Pour the beaten eggs into the pan. Let them sit undisturbed for a few seconds to begin cooking around the edges.
  7. Scramble Eggs: Using a spatula or wooden spoon, gently push the eggs from the edges toward the center of the pan. Continue to do this every few seconds, allowing the uncooked egg to flow to the bottom of the pan.
  8. Control Heat: Adjust the heat as needed to ensure the eggs cook evenly and don't brown too quickly. It's important to keep the heat gentle to achieve soft, creamy scrambled eggs.
  9. Finish Cooking: Continue to gently scramble the eggs until they are softly set but still slightly moist. They should have a creamy texture with no visible liquid egg remaining.
  10. Serve Immediately: Transfer the scrambled eggs to a plate and serve them hot. They pair well with toast, bacon, sausage, or fresh fruit for a complete breakfast. Enjoy!
